From Victoria to the Nation

A 130-year story of the AFL, told in stadiums, ladders, and goals. How a breakaway competition from 1897 grew into Australia's most-watched football code.

The big picture across Australia

Across 129 seasons, more than four out of every five AFL matches have been played in Victoria. The map below makes the league's centre of gravity unmistakable.

Figure 1 · Total AFL/VFL matches played in each state and territory, 1897 to 2025. Source: AFL Tables match records.

Premiership cups, unevenly shared

Sixteen clubs have lifted the cup in 128 grand finals. Three Melbourne foundation clubs (Collingwood, Carlton and Essendon) share nearly half of all premierships between them.

Many of the newer interstate clubs have just one or two flags. Some, like Gold Coast and GWS, have none yet. The chart on the right ranks every club that has won at least one premiership.

The league grew over time

The VFL began with eight Melbourne clubs in 1897. It stayed that way for a quarter-century until three new Victorian clubs joined in 1925.

Real expansion only came in 1987, when West Coast and Brisbane became the first non-Victorian sides. Five more interstate clubs followed over the next 25 years, taking the modern AFL to 18 teams.

How the game itself has changed

Three views of how AFL has evolved over 130 years: how high-scoring matches have become, how competitive results are, and how the season has grown longer.

Decades of dominance

A heatmap of every premiership decade by decade. Hawthorn's 1980s and Brisbane's early 2000s threepeat both stand out as the densest cells. Foundation clubs Carlton and Collingwood appear in nearly every decade.
