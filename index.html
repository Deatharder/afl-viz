From Victoria to the Nation

A 130-year story of the AFL, told in stadiums, ladders, and goals. How a breakaway competition from 1897 grew into Australia's most-watched football code.

The big picture across Australia

Across 129 seasons, more than four out of every five AFL matches have been played in Victoria. The map below makes the league's centre of gravity unmistakable.

Figure 1 · Total AFL/VFL matches played in each state and territory, 1897 to 2025. Source: AFL Tables match records.

Premiership cups, unevenly shared

If matches are concentrated in Victoria, premierships are even more so. Sixteen clubs have lifted the cup in 128 grand finals. Three Melbourne foundation clubs (Collingwood, Carlton and Essendon) share nearly half of all premierships between them.

Many of the newer interstate clubs have just one or two flags. Some, like Gold Coast and GWS, have none yet.

The league grew over time

The VFL began with eight Melbourne clubs in 1897 and stayed that way for a quarter-century until three new Victorian clubs joined in 1925.

Real expansion only came in 1987, when West Coast and Brisbane became the first non-Victorian sides. Five more interstate clubs followed over the next 25 years, taking the modern AFL to 18 teams.

How the game itself has changed

More clubs, more matches, and a very different style of football. Three views of how AFL has evolved over 130 years: scoring rates, match competitiveness, and season length.

Decades of dominance

Some clubs win in spurts rather than steadily. This heatmap shows premierships decade by decade. Hawthorn's 1980s and Brisbane's early 2000s threepeat stand out as the densest cells, while foundation clubs Carlton and Collingwood appear in nearly every decade.

A century of wins and losses

Plotted by their total wins against their total losses, you can see two stories at once. The dashed line marks an even record. Bubble size shows total matches played: older Melbourne clubs sit top-right, newer interstate sides cluster near the origin, and only Collingwood sits clearly below the diagonal with more wins than losses.

The home advantage

Every club wins more at home than away. Geelong's home record at Kardinia Park is the most extreme in the modern game.

How blowouts have changed

Match margins have widened with the scoring era. The tail of 60-plus point blowouts is longest in the modern game.

The rise and fall of dynasties

Premierships come in clusters, not waves. This chart traces the final ladder position of every club to win a flag since 2000. Brisbane's three-peat collapses into a decade in the bottom four.
