From Victoria to the Nation

For ninety years the AFL was the VFL: a Victorian competition where eight Melbourne clubs played out the season among themselves. In 1987 it stopped being that. This is the story of a national league still half-pretending it's a local one.

The big picture across Australia

Across 129 seasons, more than four out of every five AFL matches have been played in Victoria. The map below makes the league's centre of gravity unmistakable.

Figure 1 · Total AFL/VFL matches played in each state and territory, 1897 to 2025. Source: AFL Tables match records (akareen/AFL-Data-Analysis).

Premiership cups, unevenly shared

If matches are concentrated in Victoria, premierships are even more so. Sixteen clubs have lifted the cup in 128 grand finals. Three Melbourne foundation clubs (Collingwood, Carlton and Essendon) share nearly half of all premierships between them.

Many of the newer interstate clubs have just one or two flags. Some, like Gold Coast and GWS, have none yet. Fitzroy, the only foundation club to fold rather than relocate, retains eight flags from before its 1996 merger.

The league grew over time

The VFL began with eight Melbourne clubs in 1897 and stayed that way for a quarter-century until three new Victorian clubs joined in 1925.

Real expansion only came in 1987, when West Coast and Brisbane became the first non-Victorian sides. Five more interstate clubs followed over the next 25 years, taking the modern AFL to 18 teams.

Decades of dominance

Premierships don't come evenly. They come in dynasties. The heatmap below makes the clusters visible. Hawthorn won four flags in eight years across the 1980s. Brisbane Lions, an interstate club playing finals only after 1995, won three consecutive premierships in 2001 to 2003. The dark-bordered cells mark every decade where one club won three or more.

Figure 7 · Premierships won by each club, by decade. Empty cells indicate the club did not win that decade. Dark borders mark dynasty cells (3 or more flags in a decade).

A century of wins and losses

Plotted by their total wins against their total losses, two stories appear at once. Foundation clubs sit top-right with more than two thousand matches each. Recent interstate sides cluster near the origin. Only Collingwood sits clearly below the diagonal with more wins than losses. The two clubs that played less than three hundred matches are 1908 to 1914 University FC (defunct, the league's worst-ever record at 21%) and 2011 to today's Gold Coast Suns.

Figure 8 · Total wins versus total losses for every club to ever compete in the AFL/VFL, 1897 to 2025. Source: AFL match records.

The rise and fall of dynasties

Premierships come in clusters, not waves. This chart traces the final ladder position of every club to win a flag since 2000. Brisbane's three-peat collapses into a decade in the bottom four. Geelong rules from 2007 to 2011. Hawthorn climbs to its 2013 to 2015 three-peat. Richmond, four years from the wooden spoon in 2016, wins three flags in four years. Hover any coloured line to highlight that club's path.
